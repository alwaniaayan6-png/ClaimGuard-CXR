The Problem

AI radiology reports hallucinate where it matters most.

Large language and vision-language models are fluent but routinely invent findings that contradict the underlying imaging study. A single flipped word — "right" instead of "left", "no" instead of "mild" — can change a diagnosis, reroute a workup, or ruin an outcome. The method

Four steps. One guarantee.

Decompose, verify, calibrate, triage. Each stage is individually replaceable; the statistical guarantee only relies on exchangeability between the calibration and test distributions.

01
Decompose

Atomic claims

A rule-based extractor splits each report into sentence-level claims. One finding per claim, context-preserving.

02
Verify

RoBERTa-large

A binary cross-encoder, fine-tuned on 26,984 examples across 8 hard-negative perturbation types, scores each claim against retrieved evidence.

03
Calibrate

Inverted cfBH

Compute a conformal p-value against the contradicted calibration pool. This inversion fixes the score-saturation failure mode of standard cfBH.

04
Triage

Global BH

Apply Benjamini-Hochberg at level α to decide which claims to flag GREEN. By construction, FDR among GREEN ≤ α.

The key insight

Calibrate on the wrong ones.

Softmax scores for correctly verified claims concentrate near the ceiling (median 0.981, IQR < 0.001), yielding vacuous p-values. By calibrating against contradicted claims — whose scores cluster near 0.01 — we recover a non-degenerate reference distribution: 

p_j = (|{ i ∈ C_contra : s_i ≥ s_j }| + 1) / (|C_contra| + 1)

A small p-value means s_j is anomalously high relative to the contradicted distribution — strong evidence the claim is not hallucinated.
